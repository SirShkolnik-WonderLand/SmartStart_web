

GDPR INTERNATIONAL TRANSFER CHECKLIST (TIA-READY)
AliceSolutions Ventures Inc. — Hub & Ventures
Version: 1.0 • Primary Law: GDPR (EU/EEA) • Notes: UK/EU variants included

> Purpose. Use this checklist **before any cross-border disclosure** of personal data. It helps you decide **(a)** whether the flow is a Chapter V “transfer”, **(b)** which **transfer tool** to use (Adequacy, SCCs, DPF, IDTA, etc.), and **(c)** what **supplementary measures** are required (per EDPB Rec. 01/2020), with a built-in **Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) summary**. Keep the completed checklist with the project DPIA and ROPA. ([edpb.europa.eu][1])

---

## 0) Is this a “TRANSFER” under GDPR? (Gate)

All three must be **Yes**:

\[ ] **Exporter** is a controller/processor **subject to GDPR** for this processing (Art. 3).
\[ ] Data will be **disclosed or made available** to a **separate** controller/processor (the importer).
\[ ] The importer is in a **third country** (outside EEA) or an **international organisation** (regardless of whether the importer is itself subject to GDPR).
If **any** are **No**, Chapter V may not apply (still assess general GDPR risks). ([edpb.europa.eu][2])

Notes: For UK-origin data, apply **UK GDPR** rules and tools (IDTA or UK Addendum). ([ico.org.uk][3])

## 3) TIA — Six Steps (EDPB Rec. 01/2020)

**3.1 Know your transfers.** List all importers/onward recipients & sub-processors.
**3.2 Identify your tool.** (Adequacy, SCCs, IDTA/Addendum, DPF, or Derogation).
**3.3 Assess destination law & practices** (public-authority access, redress, importer obligations).
\*\*3.4 Adopt **supplementary measures** (technical/contractual/organisational).
**3.5 Procedural steps** (sign SCCs/IDTA, update annexes, consult DPO if high risk).
**3.6 Re-evaluate periodically** or on change signals (vendor, law, scope). ([edpb.europa.eu][11])

(For quick methodology guidance you may also reference the **CNIL TIA Guide** — optional aid aligned to the EDPB steps.) ([cnil.fr][12])

## 5) SCCs / IDTA Annex Aide (attach to contract)

**Annex I (Parties & Transfer details):** purposes, categories, frequency, retention, recipients, contact points.
**Annex II (TOMs):** crypto at rest/in transit, key mgmt/residency, access controls, logging, backups, DLP, incident response, vulnerability mgmt, secure dev/CI (SBOM, SAST/dep scan), secrets mgmt.
**Annex III (Sub-processors):** list & services; add onboarding/notification clause. ([EUR-Lex][8])

**UK:** If using **EU SCCs + UK Addendum**, ensure Addendum tables completed; or use **IDTA** standalone. ([ico.org.uk][10])

---

## 6) Special Cases

**US transfers:** If importer is **DPF-certified**, record the **DPF scope** (HR vs. non-HR data) and check list enrolment. If not certified, use **SCCs** + TIA + measures. Monitor DPF legal landscape. ([dataprivacyframework.gov][5], [EUR-Lex][4], [Reuters][7])
**Processor-to-Processor / Processor-to-Controller:** choose the right **SCC Module** and confirm controller instructions & onward limits. ([EUR-Lex][8])
**UK origin:** Apply **IDTA/Addendum**; consider UK-US **data bridge** status for context (still complete tool selection). ([ico.org.uk][10])
